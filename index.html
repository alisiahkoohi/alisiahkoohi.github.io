Research interests

My research, under the mentorship of Maarten V. de Hoop and Richard G. Baraniuk, bridges the gap between AI and computational science, focusing on designing reliable AI models by quantifying their uncertainty and incorporating domain-specific knowledge, e.g., system invariances, physical principles, and mathematical constraints. Primarily based on generative models, variational inference, and hypernetworks, I develop methods that are designed to enhance the robustness and reliability of AI models in complex, dynamic environments with limited access to data.
